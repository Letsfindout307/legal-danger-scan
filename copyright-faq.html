Copyright Law FAQs

10 common questions about DMCA and fair use

1. Can I use memes commercially?

Risky without permission. Most memes use copyrighted images/videos. Transformative use may qualify as fair use, but:

  • No clear legal precedent for memes
  • Commercial use weighs against fair use
  • Best practice: Use original content or public domain images
2. What counts as fair use?

Four factors courts consider:

  1. Purpose: Education/commentary favored over commercial
  2. Nature: Factual works more than creative
  3. Amount: Small portions favored
  4. Effect: No market harm to original

Campbell v. Acuff-Rose, 510 U.S. 569 (1994) - Parody may qualify.

3. How much text can I quote?

No safe percentage. Courts have found infringement with as little as 300 words from a book. Safer approaches:

  • Quote only what's necessary for your point
  • Add substantial commentary/analysis
  • Attribute properly
