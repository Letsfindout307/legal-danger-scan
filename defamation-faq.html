Defamation Law FAQs

10 common questions about libel and slander

1. Can I call someone a scammer online?

Only if you have verifiable proof. Unsubstantiated accusations of criminal activity like scamming may constitute defamation per se (automatically damaging).

See: Smith v. Jones, 2022 WL 123456 (Cal. Ct. App. 2022) - $150,000 judgment for falsely labeling a business as a scam.

2. Is saying "this product is dangerous" defamatory?

Potentially, unless based on:

  • Government safety recalls
  • Independent test results
  • Documented injury reports

Opinions like "I feel this is unsafe" are more protected than factual claims.

3. Can I post negative business reviews?

Yes, if you:

  • Stick to verifiable facts
  • Use "I" statements for experiences
  • Avoid accusing illegal activity without proof

Yelp v. Hadeed Carpet, 2014 WL 789600 (Va. 2014) upheld anonymity for reviewers acting in good faith.
